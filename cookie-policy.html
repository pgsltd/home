Cookie Policy

This policy explains how this website may use cookies, local storage and similar technologies, and how external services such as Google Fonts, hosted images and WhatsApp may process technical information.

The current version of this website is designed without advertising cookies, behavioural profiling or visitor-tracking analytics. This must be reassessed before any analytics tool, advertising pixel, embedded video, live chat service or similar technology is added.

1. What are cookies and similar technologies?

Cookies are small text files that websites may place on a browser or device. They can help a website function, remember a user’s choices, maintain security, understand use of a service or support advertising.

Similar technologies can include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, device identifiers, scripts and other methods that store information on, or access information from, a user’s device.

2. How the current website is configured

The current Planning Gain Specialist Ltd website is intended to operate as a primarily static informational website. Its main functions are:

  • displaying information about commercial and domestic construction services;
  • providing navigation between pages;
  • showing the company’s contact details;
  • opening WhatsApp links; and
  • formatting quotation-form information into a WhatsApp message for the visitor to review and send.

The website does not currently use a website account system, online payment system, advertising network, behavioural profiling tool or built-in visitor analytics platform.

The quotation form runs in the visitor’s browser. It does not, by itself, submit the completed form to a website database. When the visitor submits the form, the website prepares a WhatsApp message and opens WhatsApp or WhatsApp Web.

4. External services used by the website

Google Fonts

The website currently loads font files from Google Fonts. When the browser requests those files, Google may receive technical information such as the device’s IP address, browser details and the page making the request.

Externally hosted images

Some website images may be loaded from external image-hosting services. The image provider may receive technical connection information when the browser loads an image.

WhatsApp

Links and quotation buttons can open WhatsApp or WhatsApp Web. Once a visitor chooses to open or send a message through WhatsApp, the interaction is processed under WhatsApp’s own terms and privacy practices.

Website hosting

The website host may process basic server logs for security, reliability and delivery of the website. These logs may include an IP address, date and time, requested page, browser information and error details.

Planning Gain Specialist Ltd does not control all technologies independently used by third-party providers. Visitors should review the relevant provider’s privacy and cookie information where appropriate.

5. When consent is required

Under UK rules, technologies that are not exempt generally require clear and informed consent before they are used. Consent must involve a genuine choice and a positive action. Merely continuing to browse is not treated as valid consent for non-essential tracking.

Technologies that are strictly necessary to provide a service specifically requested by the visitor may be used without consent, although clear information should still be provided where appropriate.

If this website later introduces non-essential analytics, advertising tools, embedded media or other non-exempt technologies, those tools should be prevented from operating until the visitor has made an appropriate choice.

6. Managing your choices

You can control cookies and related storage through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • view stored cookies;
  • delete some or all cookies;
  • block cookies from particular websites;
  • block third-party cookies;
  • clear local or session storage; and
  • configure notifications when storage technologies are used.

Blocking all cookies or scripts may affect the operation of some websites. Browser controls do not necessarily prevent information being transmitted when external resources are requested, so privacy-focused browser settings or content-blocking tools may provide additional control.

7. Changes to website technologies

This policy should be updated whenever the website’s technology changes. In particular, a new review is required before adding:

  • Google Analytics, Microsoft Clarity or another analytics service;
  • Meta Pixel, Google Ads tags or other advertising tools;
  • embedded YouTube, Vimeo, maps, social-media feeds or review widgets;
  • live chat or customer-support platforms;
  • online payments, booking systems or customer accounts; or
  • any tool that stores information on, or accesses information from, a visitor’s device.

Where required, a suitable consent banner and preference-management tool should be implemented before those technologies are activated.
