Privacy Policy

2. Personal information we may collect

Depending on how you interact with the company, information may include:

  • your name, telephone number, email address and correspondence details;
  • company or organisation name and your role;
  • project address, postcode and property information;
  • details about proposed construction, renovation, maintenance or related works;
  • budget range, anticipated timescale, planning status and drawing availability;
  • photographs, plans, specifications, messages and documents you choose to provide;
  • records of enquiries, quotations, appointments, discussions and project correspondence;
  • technical information made available by your browser or device, such as IP address, browser type, referring page and basic access logs, where the website host or external service collects it; and
  • information required to manage a contract, payment, legal obligation, complaint or dispute.

Please avoid sending special-category or highly sensitive personal information unless it is genuinely necessary and the company has asked you to provide it securely.

3. How information is collected

Information may be collected:

  • directly from you through WhatsApp, telephone calls, correspondence, meetings or site visits;
  • when you complete the website’s project enquiry form;
  • from another person involved in the project, such as a property owner, contractor, consultant or authorised representative;
  • from public registers, planning records or other lawful public sources where relevant to a project; and
  • automatically through website hosting logs and third-party resources loaded by the website.

The current quotation form does not itself send information to a website database. It formats the information entered and opens a WhatsApp message for you to review and send. Information typed into the form may still exist temporarily in your browser, and information sent through WhatsApp is processed through WhatsApp’s service.

5. Who information may be shared with

Where necessary and lawful, information may be shared with:

  • employees, authorised representatives and professional advisers;
  • subcontractors, specialist trades, suppliers and consultants involved in assessing or delivering a project;
  • architects, engineers, surveyors, planning professionals or building-control bodies where relevant;
  • accountants, insurers, legal advisers, banks and payment providers;
  • website hosting, communications, document-storage and IT-service providers;
  • public authorities, regulators, courts, law-enforcement bodies or other parties where disclosure is required or legally justified; and
  • a prospective purchaser or successor if the business or relevant assets are sold, reorganised or transferred.

Only information reasonably necessary for the relevant purpose should be shared. Service providers may process information under contractual or legal responsibilities.

6. International services and transfers

Some third-party services used by the website or for communications may process information outside the United Kingdom. This can include WhatsApp and providers supporting web fonts, image delivery, hosting or communications.

Where the company controls an international transfer, it will seek to use a lawful transfer mechanism and appropriate safeguards where required. Separate third-party services operate under their own privacy terms and may determine aspects of their own processing.

7. How long information is kept

Information is kept only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including to meet contractual, tax, accounting, insurance, safety and legal requirements.

  • unsuccessful or preliminary enquiries may normally be reviewed and deleted when they are no longer required;
  • quotation and project records may be retained for the period needed to manage the relationship and any potential legal claim;
  • contract, invoice and accounting information may be retained for the period required by applicable financial and tax rules; and
  • information relevant to warranties, disputes, safety or legal proceedings may be kept for longer where justified.

Retention periods may vary according to the nature, value, risk and legal context of the project.

8. Information security

Reasonable organisational and technical measures are used to protect personal information against accidental loss, unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure or misuse. Access should be limited to people who need the information for legitimate business purposes.

No internet, messaging or storage system can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Do not use the website form or ordinary messaging to send passwords, bank-card details or unusually sensitive documents.

9. Your data-protection rights

Depending on the circumstances, UK data-protection law may give you rights to:

  • request access to your personal information;
  • ask for inaccurate or incomplete information to be corrected;
  • request deletion of information in certain circumstances;
  • request restriction of processing;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests or to direct marketing;
  • receive certain information in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent where consent is the lawful basis; and
  • challenge certain solely automated decisions, where applicable.

These rights are not absolute and may be limited by exemptions or overriding legal obligations. The company may need to verify your identity before responding. Requests should be made using the contact details in section 1.

10. Direct marketing

The company will not use the website enquiry form as automatic permission to send unrelated electronic marketing. Where promotional messages are sent, the company will use an appropriate lawful basis and comply with applicable electronic-communications rules. You may object to direct marketing or withdraw marketing consent at any time.

11. Cookies, fonts, images and external links

The website may use strictly necessary browser functions and may load resources from third parties, including Google Fonts, external image-hosting services and WhatsApp links. When these resources are loaded or opened, the relevant provider may receive technical information such as your IP address, browser details, device information and the referring page.

The current website is not intended to use advertising cookies or behavioural analytics. If non-essential cookies, analytics, advertising tools or embedded media are introduced, the cookie information and consent mechanism should be updated before those tools are activated.

External websites and services have their own privacy practices. This policy does not control how those independent organisations use information.

12. Questions and complaints

Please contact Planning Gain Specialist Ltd first if you have a concern about how your personal information has been handled. Contact can be made through WhatsApp or by writing to the correspondence address in section 1.

You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office. Current complaint procedures and contact information are available through the ICO’s official website.

13. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when the website, services, legal requirements or data-processing practices change. The latest version will be published on this page with a revised “last updated” date.
