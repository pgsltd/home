How we work

A clear route from first conversation to final handover.

Every construction project benefits from a defined scope, transparent communication and carefully coordinated delivery. Our process is designed to help clients understand what happens at each stage.

Project journey

Eight practical stages for controlled project delivery.

The exact process may vary according to the property, project size, information available and work required, but the following structure provides a clear starting point.

Discuss Your Project
01

Initial Enquiry

We begin by understanding the type of property, proposed work, location, preferred timescale and the best way to contact you.

  • Commercial or residential project
  • Brief description of the required work
  • Photographs, drawings or supporting information
02

Preliminary Discussion

The project is discussed in more detail so that the likely scope, constraints and next steps can be considered before a site review.

  • Project objectives and priorities
  • Access and occupancy considerations
  • Available planning or technical documents
03

Site Review

Where appropriate, the property or site is reviewed to better understand dimensions, current condition, access and the practical requirements of the work.

  • Existing condition review
  • Access and logistics
  • Potential sequencing considerations
04

Scope & Quotation

The proposed work is defined and a quotation can be prepared around the agreed scope and the information available at that stage.

  • Description of proposed work
  • Material and labour considerations
  • Assumptions and exclusions where relevant
05

Planning & Scheduling

Once the work is agreed, the project is prepared for delivery through sequencing, resource planning, material coordination and access arrangements.

  • Indicative programme
  • Trade and material coordination
  • Site access and working arrangements
06

Construction Delivery

Work progresses in line with the agreed project requirements, with coordination between the relevant activities and communication throughout delivery.

  • Managed work stages
  • Progress communication
  • Site organisation and coordination
07

Changes & Variations

Where the client requests additional work or unforeseen conditions affect the agreed scope, the implications should be discussed before the revised work proceeds.

  • Clear description of the change
  • Cost and programme implications
  • Client agreement before proceeding
08

Completion & Handover

The completed work is reviewed, any agreed outstanding items are identified, and the project is prepared for handover to the client.

  • Completion review
  • Outstanding-item discussion
  • Final project handover

02

Frequently asked questions

Common questions from prospective clients.

What types of projects can Planning Gain Specialist Ltd undertake?

The company can advertise commercial and domestic construction services, including new builds, extensions, conversions, major refurbishments, structural alterations, maintenance and restoration. The suitability of a particular project should be confirmed after discussing its scope and requirements.

Do you provide quotations?

A quotation can be considered once sufficient information is available about the property and the proposed work. Some projects may require a site review, drawings, specifications or further technical information before an accurate scope can be prepared.

Is a site visit always required?

Not every initial discussion requires an immediate site visit, but many construction, renovation and structural projects benefit from an on-site review before the scope and quotation are finalised.

Can you work from architectural drawings?

Yes, drawings, specifications, planning documents and structural information can help define the required work. Their availability should be mentioned during the initial enquiry.

Can you help if planning permission has not yet been obtained?

The construction scope can still be discussed, but any work requiring planning permission, building-control approval or other consent should not proceed until the necessary requirements have been addressed by the appropriate professionals and authorities.

How are changes to the agreed work handled?

Additional work, client-requested changes or unforeseen conditions should be recorded as variations. Their effect on cost, materials and timescale should be discussed and agreed before the changed work proceeds.

Can work be completed in an occupied property?

Some projects can be delivered in occupied homes or operating commercial premises, but access, safety, working hours, dust, noise and sequencing need to be assessed for the specific site.

Do you provide all specialist trades?

The company can offer or coordinate broader building works including roofing, plastering, flooring, decorating, joinery, kitchens and bathrooms where these form part of the agreed project. The exact trade package should be confirmed for each enquiry.

How long will my project take?

Timescales depend on the scope, property condition, access, approvals, material availability, specialist work and any variations. A project-specific programme can only be discussed after the requirements have been reviewed.

What should I send with my first enquiry?

Please provide the project address, property type, description of work, photographs, any drawings or approvals, your preferred timescale and the best contact details. This helps the company understand the enquiry more efficiently.

Information on this page explains the general project approach. Exact terms, responsibilities, timescales and deliverables should be confirmed in the project-specific quotation or agreement.
